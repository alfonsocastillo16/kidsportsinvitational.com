Coach’s Corner! An Interview with Greg Menegat

Brooke Baum
Jonathan Mills

February 25, 2014

Coach’s Corner!
An Interview with Greg Menegat

For the past ten years, Greg Menegat has coached youth sports in the Eugene area, as well as with KIDSPORTS. Menegat has lived in the Eugene area most of his life and was active in youth sports as a child, eventually a two-time all state player at Creswell High School and Most Valuable Player at Menlo College in 1987. This will be Menegat’s second year coaching his twin boys at the KIDSPORTS Invitational.

We caught up with Menegat to discuss his experience at the 2013 KSI.

How did you get started with KIDSPORTS and the Invitational?

My kids have always been involved in sports. I heard about the tournament through Bev and the KIDSPORTS website. It looked like something the kids would really enjoy and in retrospect they did.

What kind of impact does KIDSPORTS have in the community?

I have many fond memories from youth sports while growing up, and recognize and appreciate the discipline I developed from this participation. I think it’s very important for children to be engaged regardless of what it is. KIDSPORTS in particular really emphasizes participation, not necessarily competition. This is so important because they may not all end up being D-1 athletes but it keeps them engaged and healthy.

Do you coach during the regular season?

Yes, I coach the KIDSPORTS team with my 7th grade twin boys.

How are the KSI teams formed? Did you split up your team from the regular season?

No, the team was made by the kids at school; they chose the teams themselves.

What did the kids enjoy about the 2013 KSI? What is different about coaching 3-on-3?

As a coach, I love it because the skills you learn from playing 3-no-3 are super foundational for the 5-on-5 games. For the kids I think they dig the freedom. They were coached all season on things like zone defense, so this tournament allows for more free-reign to show what they have learned. I think it’s important for the kids to develop free thinking on the floor and get out there to see what you can do and try new things.

How did parents respond to the tournament?

I think the parents love the tournament the same way I did. It’s very exciting to be around the college players. The kids are so jazzed about being at a large venue and to have the college athletes watch them play basketball. Parents love seeing their kids light up and to know that this memory is going to stay with their kid forever.

What was most memorable and rewarding for you?

Seeing my kids play. I remember there was an Oregon Ducks basketball player on the side watching their game; seeing their faces while they played, knowing he was watching them, was cool. As an adult, I have very fond memories of participating in youth sports at Mac Court, so being in a venue like Matthew Knight Arena is a tremendous experience for the kids. Playing on the same court as their heroes is very exciting. I know this fosters a lot of excitement for the game and I think it is very important for them because not everyone is going to be an NBA player. But maybe down the road one of them is going to make a decision to stay active in KIDSPORTS or basketball because of this tournament.

What is the biggest advice you would give to youth basketball players?

Participation. I coach a lot of kids that aren’t super high level, but I would encourage anyone to go out and participate – try even if you aren’t the best, as opposed to not trying it. The one thing I ask all kids is to give it 100 percent. No matter what you are doing, go all out. It defines success in life, not just basketball- giving it your all.

Coach Menegat plans to coach his twins’ team again this year. We look forward to seeing them on the court in April!
